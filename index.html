Up to ₹25,000
Direct purchase
GFR Rule 154 / GeM Rule 149(i): purchase without quotation or bidding, with a reasonableness certificate by the competent authority.
₹25k – ₹2.5L
Purchase committee
GFR Rule 155: via a Local Purchase Committee. On GeM, from the lowest-priced seller of at least three makers.
GeM first
Mandatory for listed items
Common goods/services on GeM must be bought through GeM (Rule 149). We focus on custom, fabricated and specialised items.

Rules & regulations

Your rights and the law, in plain terms.

Work on YourWorkDo follows Indian labour law and the country’s criminal law — which since 1 July 2024 is the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, replacing the old Indian Penal Code (IPC).

Wages

Minimum wage & on-time pay

Every worker must be paid at least the minimum wage notified for that state and skill, and paid on time. No illegal deductions.

Code on Wages, 2019 · (Minimum Wages Act, 1948)
Hours

Working hours & overtime

Normal work is about 8–9 hours a day and 48 a week. Work beyond that is overtime, usually paid at double the normal rate.

Factories Act · State Shops & Establishments Acts
Freedom

No forced or bonded labour

No one can be forced to work against their will or trapped in debt-bondage. Workers are free to accept or decline any job.

Constitution Art. 23 · Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act, 1976
Children

No child labour

No child under 14 may be engaged for work. We never list or accept tasks that put minors to work.

Child & Adolescent Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Act, 1986 (amended 2016)
Gig workers

Social security for platform workers

Platform and gig workers are formally recognised, with welfare and registration (e-Shram). Some states levy a welfare contribution on platforms.

Code on Social Security, 2020 (in force Nov 2025) · Karnataka & Rajasthan gig-worker laws
Honesty

Fraud & breach of trust are crimes

Cheating a customer or worker, or misusing money or property entrusted to you, is a criminal offence — protecting both sides of every job.

BNS, 2023 — Cheating §318 · Criminal breach of trust §316 (replacing IPC §420 / §405–409)

This is general information to help you understand your rights, not legal advice. Exact wage rates, limits and rules vary by state and change over time — check the latest official notification or a qualified advisor for your specific case.
